    Separation of Power
    It is generally considered a best practice to keep the roles of the Board of Directors and the Officers separate to prevent conflicts of interest and ensure proper oversight. Allowing the Board to appoint themselves as Officers creates a concentration of power and limits the checks and balances within the organization.
    The Board of Directors:
    • The Board of Directors is the governing body of the association, typically consisting of elected or appointed individuals from the community. The number of members on the board and their specific roles may be outlined in the association's bylaws.
    • The primary function of the Board is to set policies, make major decisions, and oversee the general affairs of the association. They have the authority to create and enforce rules and regulations that govern the community and are responsible for the long-term management and financial well-being of the association.
    • Board members usually serve for specific terms, and their positions may be subject to election or appointment by the association's members.
    The Officers:
    • The Officers are individuals who are elected or appointed by the Board of Directors to hold specific leadership positions.
    • The most common Officer positions include President, Vice President, Secretary, and Treasurer.
    • The Officers are responsible for the day-to-day operations of the association and implementing the policies and decisions set by the Board of Directors.
    • They are accountable for managing the association's finances, overseeing community maintenance and services, handling administrative tasks, and interacting with residents and vendors on a regular basis.
